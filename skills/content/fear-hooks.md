# Fear Hook Bank

> **INSTRUCTIONS FOR CLAUDE**: Pick hooks from this bank or create close variations.
> These are for the FEAR content angle — clips show the persona worried/distressed.
> Never repeat the same hook on the same account within 14 days.
> Never use the same hook on two accounts the same day.
> Always match the hook to the persona's voice (check persona files).
> Key difference from discovery: NO phone personification. This is about the USER confronting their own behavior.

## Pattern 1: Screen Time Math (Top Performer)

Structure: "[specific stat]. [scaled-up stat]. [one-word gut punch]."
Why it works: Specific numbers feel personal. Scaling from daily → yearly makes the waste undeniable.

### ManifestLock variants
1. "7 hrs a day. 106 days a year. gone."
2. "i've spent 2,555 hours scrolling this year. and set 0 goals."
3. "my screen time this week: 49 hours. my sleep: 38."
4. "4 years of my life. scrolling. that's what the math says."
5. "i average 9 hours a day. that's more than i sleep."
6. "2,190 hours a year on my phone. 0 on what actually matters."

### JournalLock variants
7. "6 hours a day. 91 days a year. not one second journaling."
8. "i scroll for 2,190 hours a year. i reflect for maybe 2."
9. "my screen time last week was 52 hours. my self-reflection: 0."
10. "8 hrs a day. 121 days a year. and i wonder why i feel empty."
11. "i've spent 3 years of my life doom scrolling. three. years."
12. "my phone says 7.5 hours average. my journal says 0 entries this month."

## Pattern 2: Pickup Count (High Engagement)

Structure: "i pick up my phone [X] times a day. need it [0/low number] times."
Why it works: The gap between pickups and actual need is viscerally relatable.

### ManifestLock variants
13. "i pick up my phone 96 times a day. need it maybe 5."
14. "144 pickups yesterday. couldn't name one reason."
15. "i check my phone every 6 minutes. for nothing."
16. "86 pickups before noon. i hadn't even set a single goal."

### JournalLock variants
17. "i pick up my phone 112 times a day. put my thoughts down 0 times."
18. "96 pickups yesterday. 0 moments of reflection."
19. "i unlock my phone 130 times a day. i've never once opened a journal."
20. "every 7 minutes i check my phone. never once checked in with myself."

## Pattern 3: Wasted Goals / Potential (Emotional)

Structure: "[ambitious thing i planned]. [didn't do it]. screen time: [damning number]."
Why it works: Juxtaposing goals vs screen time creates immediate self-recognition.

### ManifestLock variants
21. "wrote 14 goals in january. finished none. screen time: 6 hrs/day."
22. "said i'd manifest every morning. it's march. screen time: 8 hours."
23. "i have a vision board on my wall and 47 hours of tiktok this week."
24. "bought 3 self-help books. read 0 pages. scrolled 2,190 hours."

### JournalLock variants
25. "therapist said journal daily. it's been 3 months. screen time: 7 hrs."
26. "said i'd start journaling in january. it's march. screen time: 52 hrs/week."
27. "i have a $30 journal collecting dust and 8 hours of daily screen time."
28. "everyone says write it down. i scroll instead. every single day."

## Pattern 4: Social Confrontation (Viral Potential)

Structure: "[person] [calculated/showed me] [devastating phone stat]"
Why it works: Third-party confrontation adds social proof and shareability.

### ManifestLock variants
29. "my sister calculated i scroll more than i sleep. she was right."
30. "my roommate showed me my screen time report. we both went quiet."
31. "my therapist asked me to track my screen time for a week. i cried."
32. "my best friend sent me my own screen time screenshot. didn't talk for a day."

35. "my roommate asked what i do for 8 hours on my phone. i had no answer."
36. "someone asked when i last journaled. i said 'i think about it sometimes.'"
