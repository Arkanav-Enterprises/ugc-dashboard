# Fear Caption Formulas

## The Role (Fear Angle)

Fear captions are CONFESSIONAL. The viewer just watched someone confront their screen time / scrolling habit. The caption deepens that moment of recognition.

Key differences from discovery captions:
- No "check this out" energy
- No phone-as-character framing
- First person confrontation, not third person curiosity
- Understated resolution, not excited revelation

## Caption Structure (Fear)

```
[The moment — what you calculated/realized/were told]
[1-2 sentences sitting with the feeling]
[What changed — without naming the app directly]
[Understated result — NOT a CTA]

[Hashtags]
```

Total length: 80-150 characters before hashtags. Shorter hits harder for this angle.

## Formula 1: The Calculation

```
i did the math on my screen time.
[specific stat that hurts].
so i [small action that changed things].
[quiet result].
```

**Example (ManifestLock):**
i did the math on my screen time. 106 days this year. gone.
so i made my phone ask me one question every morning before it unlocks.
it's been 3 weeks. i don't even miss the scrolling.

**Example (JournalLock):**
i calculated my screen time vs my journaling time. 2,190 hours vs 0.
so i made my phone lock until i write one thought.
it takes 90 seconds. and i feel lighter every day.

## Formula 2: Someone Told Me

```
[person] said something i couldn't unhear.
"[the quote or stat they shared]"
i [small change].
[result — understated].
```

**Example (ManifestLock):**
my therapist said "you spend 7 hours a day on something that gives you nothing back."
i started spending 3 minutes a morning on something that does.
it's not dramatic. but i feel different.

**Example (JournalLock):**
my roommate asked when i last sat with my own thoughts. i couldn't answer.
now my phone asks me the same question every morning.
turns out 2 minutes of honesty changes your whole day.

## Formula 3: The Quiet Confession

```
i wasn't going to talk about this.
but [honest admission about phone use].
[what i tried — vague enough to create curiosity].
[small result that speaks for itself].
```

**Example (ManifestLock):**
i wasn't going to post this. but my screen time was 9 hours a day.
i tried something that takes 3 minutes every morning.
4 weeks in. screen time is at 4. goals are actually happening.

**Example (JournalLock):**
honestly embarrassed to say this. i scrolled more than i slept.
one small change to my morning. that's it.
i don't even recognize my screen time report anymore.

## Formula 4: The Before/After (No Drama)

```
[before stat — raw, no editorializing].
[after stat — equally raw].
[one sentence about what changed — not what app].
```

**Example (ManifestLock):**
before: 8.5 hrs/day. 0 goals set. 0 completed.
now: 4 hrs/day. 3 minutes of intention every morning.
one lock screen changed everything.

**Example (JournalLock):**
before: 7 hrs/day screen time. 0 minutes with my own thoughts.
now: 3.5 hrs/day. 2 minutes of journaling before i can scroll.
smallest change i've ever made. biggest difference.

## What NEVER Works (Fear Captions)

- "Download this app!" or any direct CTA
- Excited/amazed tone (wrong energy for fear angle)
- Phone personification (that's discovery angle territory)
- Vague stats ("too much screen time" — always use specific numbers)
- Preachy advice ("you should reduce your screen time")
- Multiple paragraphs (fear captions should be SHORT)
